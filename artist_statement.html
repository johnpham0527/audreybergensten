Artist's Statement

Many of these paintings have figures, or heads, though some are pure landscape. The people in the paintings are not specific individuals, but instead, are pared-down representations of the human form, suggesting totems or masks.

I began 35 years ago with small mythic and poetic images. Over the years, the sizes increased and the figures developed into symbols. They have psychological aspects, but are also visual explorations. Current interests include evolution biology, and environmental effects. The focus remains the figure and its relationship to the natural world.
